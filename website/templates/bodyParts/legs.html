I : Hamstrings

DB Romanian Deadlift

1. Stand up tall with one dumbbell in each hand, palms facing you.

2. Engage your abs, and hinge your hips back as you slowly lower the dumbbells toward the middle of your shins.

3. Stand back up, then squeeze your glutes at the top.

Walking Lunges

1. Stand upright with your feet shoulder-width apart and your hands on your hips.

2. Step forward with your right leg, placing your foot down as if you were setting up a static lunge, flexing your knees (90°), and dropping your hips.

3. Lower your left knee toward the ground.

4. Just before it makes contact with the floor, drive up and forward through your right leg, stepping into a lunge on your other side.

Rear-Foot Elevated Lunge

1. Place your right foot on a bench or step, keeping your toes pointed, your foot flexed, and pressure penetrating the ball of your left foot [and the top of your right foot].

2. Once in position, descend under control until your right knee just about touches the floor and drive back up through your left leg to the starting position.

3. Make sure your back knee doesn’t collapse toward your body, and that your forward knee doesn’t slide past your toes.

II : Calves

Standing Calf Raises

1. With hands at the side, toes pointed forward and a straight knee.

2. Raise heels off the ground while pushing through the balls of feet.

3. Use control throughout the movement with a slight pause at the top of the contraction.

Seated DB Calf Raise

1. Place the weight plate on the floor. Sit at the end of a bench and rest your toes on top of the weight plate.

2. Put a dumbbell on your knee, holding the handle with your right hand while your left hand holds the top.

3. Raise your toes as high as you can without lifting them off the plate. Hold for a moment, lower your toes, and repeat.

Farmer’s Walk (on Tiptoes)

1. Pick up a pair of dumbbells or kettlebells and hold them at your sides.

2. Get on your tippy-toes and walk forward as fast as you can for as far as you can go, taking short steps.

3. Use a lighter weight to sustain balance and add more weight as you progress.

III : Quads

Hack Squat

1. Your stance on the foot platform will closely mimic that of your back squat stance.

2. Your torso should be stable with your abdominals engaged and your lower back flat on the back pad.

3. Maintain a neutral head position as you lower your body until the bottoms of your thighs are parallel to the foot platform and drive through your heels to the top.

Leg Press

1. Place your feet on the sled of a leg press, matching your standard squat stance if possible.

2. Press the sled out of the rack, lower the safety bars, and then lower the sled towards your chest until your thighs break 90 degrees.

3. Press the sled back up. Do not lock out your knees fully at the top of the movement.

Leg Extensions

1. Adjust the back pad on the machine such that the back of your knee fits snugly against the edge of the seat.

2. Extend the knee to move the ankle pad, until your knee is fully straightened.

3. Lower the weight back down with control.
